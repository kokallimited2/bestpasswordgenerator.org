Cookie Policy

What cookies we use, why, and how to manage them.

Last updated: 6 May 2026

Good news: We use a very minimal set of cookies — far fewer than most websites. Our password generator tool requires no cookies at all. Optional analytics cookies are only set with your explicit consent.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored in your browser when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and how you use them. This site also uses localStorage (browser storage that never transmits data to servers) for storing your consent preference locally.

Cookie Categories Explained

Essential cookies

Required for the site to function. Cannot be disabled. These include storing your consent preference and browser-cached font files. The password generator itself requires no cookies — all randomness generation runs in your browser without any external calls.

Analytics cookies (optional)

Only set if you click "Accept All" on our cookie banner. These help us understand which pages are most useful, where readers come from, and how to improve content. No personally identifiable information is included in analytics data.

Third-party / affiliate cookies

When you click an affiliate link, the destination site may set its own cookies to track the referral. These are governed by the third party's own cookie and privacy policies. We have no control over these cookies.

How to Delete Cookies

You can delete or block cookies at any time through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Clear browsing data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Clear Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Clear browsing data

To clear localStorage specifically, open your browser's Developer Tools (F12), go to Application → Local Storage → bestpasswordgenerator.org, and delete the bpg-ck key. The cookie banner will reappear on your next visit.
