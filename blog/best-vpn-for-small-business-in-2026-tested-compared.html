Introduction: Why Your Business Needs a VPN in 2026

Remote work isn't going anywhere. In 2026, 68% of small businesses have at least some remote employees, and that number keeps climbing. But here's the problem: every remote connection is a potential security hole.

Without a business VPN, your team is:

  • ❌ Accessing company files over unsecured home WiFi
  • ❌ Exposing sensitive data to coffee shop network snoops
  • ❌ Creating compliance nightmares for regulated industries
  • ❌ Making it impossible to enforce consistent security policies

A business VPN solves all of this by creating encrypted tunnels for all work traffic, centralizing access control, and giving you visibility into who's doing what.

In this our blog guide, we tested 4 leading business VPNs across 12 criteria to find the best option for different business needs.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a consumer VPN for my business?

A: You can, but you shouldn't for teams. Consumer VPNs lack centralized management, user controls, activity monitoring, and business-grade support. For 2+ employees, invest in a business VPN.

Q: What's the difference between a business VPN and remote desktop?

A: A VPN encrypts all internet traffic from a device. Remote desktop (like TeamViewer, RDP) lets you control a computer remotely. They complement each other — VPN secures the connection, remote desktop provides access.

Q: Will a VPN slow down my internet?

A: Some speed loss is normal (10-25%) due to encryption overhead. Our tests showed 78-86% speed retention — more than fast enough for video calls, file sharing, and cloud apps.

Q: Do I need a dedicated IP?

A: Yes, for most businesses. A dedicated IP lets you: - Whitelist your office IP for secure resource access - Avoid CAPTCHAs and security blocks - Set up secure site-to-site connections - Maintain consistent access for compliance

Q: Can I switch VPNs easily?

A: Yes, but plan the transition: 1. Set up the new VPN alongside the old one 2. Migrate users in batches 3. Update firewall rules and whitelists 4. Decommission the old VPN

Most providers offer migration assistance for teams of 10+.

Q: Is a VPN enough for remote work security?

A: No. A VPN is essential but not sufficient. You also need: - Endpoint protection (antivirus/EDR) - Multi-factor authentication - Employee security training - Regular security audits - Data backup and recovery plans

Q: What about free VPNs for business?

A: Never use free VPNs for business. They often: - Sell your browsing data - Inject ads into traffic - Have weak or no encryption - Lack essential security features - Provide no support or accountability

The $4-12/month per user for a business VPN is minimal compared to the cost of a data breach.
